Hartbeesport Dam

Situated amongst the Magaliesburg Mountain Range, Hartbeespoort Dam is a popular getaway for those looking to enjoy the outdoor beauty of the Highveld. Water sports enthusiasts can enjoy water skiing, windsurfing, and jet skiing on the dam. This geologically beautiful area offers many scenic hikes of varying difficulty amongst the Magaliesburg Mountains. The Crocodile Ramble is an arts and crafts route in the area for those wanting to spend a lazy day shopping and lunching in the Highveld sunshine.

Sun City

Situated in an extinct volcano, just two hours from Johannesburg, is the Sun City Resort. The Resort features four hotels including the Palace of the Lost City, a glorious African fantasy world filled with waterfalls and tumbling gardens. Sun City features the most advanced water park in South Africa, the Valley of the Waves. There are two international 18 hole golf courses in the Resort including the Gary Player Country Club which hosts the annual Nebank Challenge. Next to the Resort is the Pilanesburg National Park where you can view the Big 5.

Lesedi Cultural Village

Just an hour away from Johannesburg in the Cradle of Humankind is the Lesedi Cultural Village. Learn about the Zulu, Xhosa, Pedi, Basotho, and Ndebele with different sections of the village devoted to each cultural group. Representatives from each of the tribes were involved in the building of the different cultural villages, and much care was taken in order to make sure that each representation is as historically correct as possible. Members from the different communities now live in Lesedi, helping to breathe life into their cultures. Guests to the Lesedi Cultural Village are treated to a tour of the five different cultural areas, as well as traditional food and dancing. The aim of the village is to highlight the differences in the cultures and to showcase to a wider audience the interesting and beautiful cultures of South Africa. Kingsley Holgate, the famous National Geographic Explorer, is one of the directors of the Lesedi Cultural Village and is passionate about preserving African cultures in a world that is becoming more and more Westernised. Visitors are able to enjoy the music, song and warmth of Africa, and get to explore the different cultures of South Africa and learn about their interesting histories and traditions.

Klerksdorp Museum

Orkney Golf Club

This 18 hole golf course was officially opened in 1939. It was originally a 9-hole course and the second 9 were only introduced in the mid-forties. It was built by the famous golf club architect Bob Grimsdell. Orkney has produced top stars such as Simon Hobday and Jannie Le Grange. There have been many long standing trophies here, including the Pollak Cup, Directors Cup and Haggard Cup. Conference facilities are available for hire which can accommodate 50 people, as well as a function hall which is ideal for weddings, year-end functions, parties and other special events. The function hall can accommodate about 200 people. There is a restaurant on the premises, with a big screen, pool table and bar. There is also a fully stocked pro-shop with a wide range of impressive products and gear. Dress code is smart casual.

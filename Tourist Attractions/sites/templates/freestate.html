National Museum

Established in 1877 the National Museum of South Africa stands as an international institution, focussing on natural and cultural history and art. The museum's paleantology and archaeology section has many interesting exhibits and seeks to show visitors what the world was like millions of years ago. The only complete skeleton of the Euskelosaurus, the earliest known plant-eating dinosaur, is on display at the museum. The Oliewenhuis Art Museum is part of the National Museum and is aimed at conserving and collecting works of art that represent the heritage of South African art. Guided tours through the museum are available, or browse the many exhibits at your leisure.

Bagamoya Wildlife Estate

Located in Motheo in Bloemfontein, a visit to the Bagamoya Wildlife Estate is a unique experience and allows you to interact with large cats including lions, tigers and much more. This is a truly African experience where you can enjoy game drives, lion feeding, the Lioness restaurant, kids play area and lots of other outdoor activities. While visiting the estate you may come across 28 different types of buck, giraffe, hippo, lama, camels, lions, tigers, bat-eared fox and lots more. Open Wednesdays to Sundays. Entrance Fees: Adults R20, kids under 10 years old R25. Game drive fees: R200 per adult and R100 per child. For more info, go to www.bagamoyawildlifeestate.co.za or call +27 (0) 82 497 7550

Liesel Wessels Art Gallery

Liesel Wessels is a South African fine artist located in Bloemfontein. She specialises in portraits, nudes and other types of figures. She has been creating serious art since 2002 and constantly strives to break through walls of limitations to reach and create better artwork. The gallery contains portraits, nudes, landscapes, still life, photography, abstracts, ceramics and more. For more info, go to www.lieselwessels.co.za or call +27 (0) 83 400 2864

Orchid House

The Orchid House is located in Hamilton Park, Bloemfontein. It is a glass domed orchid garden nursery which features an exotic collection of hundreds of these rare and beautiful flowers. The best time to view the modern nursery and garden is in winter as this is when orchids are in bloom. For more info, call +27 (0) 51 412 7017

Free State National Botanical Gardens

Stretching over 70 hectares, the Free State NBG is located in a valley with a number of dolerite koppies marking the landscape. A meandering path takes you through the gardens past wild olive and karee trees, grassland and woodland, and a petrified tree that is thought to be over 150 million years old. The best time to enjoy the gardens is in spring when the flowers are in bloom. The gardens also feature two displays showing medicinal plants, and another demonstrating water-wise gardening. The Botanical Society organizes summer sunset concerts, moonlight walks, and educational talks.
